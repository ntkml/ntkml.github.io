Who I Am

Nthabiseng, but you can call me Nthabi...

The Basics: Nthabiseng Kamala. 21. Aquarius.

The name "Nthabiseng" originates from the South African language Xhosa meaning happiness or joy — an energy I try my best to continuously emulate. You can just call me Nthabi, though. I'm proud to be the daughter of two African immigrants and feel empowered carrying my culture through my name.

Moving Mountains Through Stories...

Being a black woman in America has its fair share of challenges. Growing up as a black woman, the daughter of two African immigrants, living in white, red county, rural Ohio comes with even more. Representation lacked not only in my community butin the movies/TV I watched, the books I read, and the leaders I saw. By the time I was a junior in high school and realized my worth, I had to deconstruct 17 years of self-hatred stemming from the Eurocentric ideals I was trying so desperately to subscribe to.
For the underrepresented, seeing a person on television who looks like them can have a limiting effect if that person is restricted to behaving only in certain ways, which don’t reflect the breadth of their life’s experience. I was one of those people.
Media has an incredible, instantaneous reach with a platform powerful enough to have versatile narratives seen by potentially millions. We choose the stories that are shared — and I want to create content that inspires and excites audiences with representation from all walks of life.
Media has the power to move mountains, spark movements, and elicit change. With 30 seconds, a photo, a design, a slogan — lives are impacted.

And I want to be at the front end of that.
