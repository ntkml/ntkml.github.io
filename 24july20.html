24 july 2020 - growing pains



My expectations for 2020 and what it has paned out to be are inexplicably disconnected.

The state of the world has informed how I navigate my personhood and as my personhood adapts to new normals, further growing into myself, it has also informed how I look at the world. The past five months have been dynamic, to say the least, and I’ve struggled with balancing the aches and pains of accelerated growth.


COVID-19. Sent home from school during my final months of college. Virtual Graduation. Lost Internship. Ahmaud Arbery. George Floyd. Breonna Taylor. Tony McDade. Nina Pop. Iyanna Dior.


My body aches. My heart hurts. My eyes shake. My blood boils. Every part of me hurts.


How do I do my part in dismantling systems that disenfranchise people that look like me and call for black liberation all while still existing within the system?


How do I make sure the work I do serves and doesn’t just cause more hurt?


I don’t have the answers for these questions yet — and I think I can go out on a limb that many of you reading this may be facing the same internal conflict.


This is a post of vulnerability to say that I’m lost and confused in the hopes that those feeling the same feel comfort.
